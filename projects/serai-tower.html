The Concept

A tower designed to
disappear into the sky.

Serai Tower's slim floor plate — just 22 metres at its narrowest — was a deliberate act of restraint. In a city that confuses width with wealth, we chose a profile that maximises creek and marina views for every apartment, not just the penthouses.

The tower's curtain wall uses a deep bronze-tinted glass that changes character throughout the day: pale gold in morning light, almost graphite at dusk. Sky terraces at levels 18 and 34 interrupt the façade, creating usable outdoor space accessible to all residents without reservation.

The four full-floor penthouses at the summit are the first Amarah residences designed with a private chef's kitchen as a standard specification, not an upgrade.
